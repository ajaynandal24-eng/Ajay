## Component 1: MBTI Result and Leadership Style (20%)

**Suggested length: ~600 words**

### Evidence Required

- [ ] Complete the [Fastest MBTI Test](https://dynomight.net/mbti)
- [ ] Include a screenshot or image of your MBTI result
- [ ] (Optional) Include your MBTI "typie" from [The Myers-Briggs Typies](https://share.themyersbriggs.com/en-US/Typies)

### What to Write

1. **State your MBTI type** (e.g., INTJ, ENFP, etc.) and briefly explain what each dimension means for you.
2. **Interpret what your type suggests about your preferred leadership style** — go beyond just describing the label; explain how the traits manifest in how you lead or prefer to be led.
3. **Link to leadership theories covered in the module**, for example:
   - **Situational Leadership Model** (Hersey & Blanchard) — how does your MBTI type relate to telling, selling, participating, or delegating styles?
   - **Transformational vs. Transactional Leadership** (Bass & Avolio)
   - **Trait Theory** — how do your MBTI traits align with identified leadership traits?
   - **Authentic Leadership** — does your type support self-awareness and relational transparency?
   - Any other relevant theories from tutor-led sessions or wider reading.

### Key Guidance

- The label itself is less important than **how well you interpret it** and **how convincingly you link it to leadership theory and practice**.
- For higher grades, go beyond core readings — draw on wider sources from the library reading list and Canvas materials.

## Component 2: Belbin Role and Teamwork Reflection (30%)

**Suggested length: ~900 words**

### Evidence Required

- [ ] Complete the [Team Roles Test](https://www.123test.com/team-roles-test/) (or use the SLW version)
- [ ] Identify your preferred Belbin role

### What to Write

#### Part A: Your Role in Teams (~200–250 words)

1. **State your strongest Belbin role** and explain what it suggests about how you typically contribute in a team.
2. Ground this in your **real behaviour**, not just the label description.
3. Use **Belbin's framework** to explain why the role fits you.

#### Part B: What Makes an Effective Team (~250–300 words)

1. If you were building a team, identify **two other Belbin roles** you would prioritise working alongside you.
2. Explain **how they complement your role** and **what they add** to team effectiveness.
3. Reference Belbin's theory on balanced teams.

#### Part C: Leadership Learning from Group Work (~350–400 words)

1. **Reflect on what you learned about leadership** from working in your SLW team while developing insights for the change plan.
2. Consider:
   - How leadership **emerged or shifted** within the group
   - How the team **made decisions**
   - How any **disagreement or conflict** was handled
3. **Use a reflective framework** to structure your reflection:
   - **Kolb's Learning Cycle** (Concrete Experience → Reflective Observation → Abstract Conceptualisation → Active Experimentation)
   - **Gibbs' Reflective Cycle** (Description → Feelings → Evaluation → Analysis → Conclusion → Action Plan)
   - Or another suitable framework
4. Support your reflection with relevant **leadership theory**, for example:
   - Transformational Leadership
   - Situational Leadership
   - Distributed Leadership
5. Make **clear links** between your test result, your lived experience in the group, and module theory.

## Component 3: Change Management Plan (50%)

**Suggested length: ~1,500 words**

### The Case

> ARU is considering adopting AI to mark student coursework assignments and exams. As part of a consultancy team, you have been asked to design an organisational change plan to inform ARU's decision and help guide implementation.

### Suggested Structure

#### 1. Introduction (~150–200 words)

- Briefly state the **purpose of the plan**.
- Define what a **"successful" implementation** would look like.

#### 2. Enablers and Barriers (~400–500 words)

Use **Lewin's Force Field Analysis** (recommended) or another suitable framework.

| Category | Factor | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Enablers (Driving Forces)** | Institutional readiness | _Your analysis_ |
| | Infrastructure | _Your analysis_ |
| | Leadership support | _Your analysis_ |
| | Student/staff benefits | _Your analysis_ |
| | Capability building | _Your analysis_ |
| **Barriers (Restraining Forces)** | Resistance to change | _Your analysis_ |
| | Workload pressures | _Your analysis_ |
| | Ethical concerns | _Your analysis_ |
| | Trust and transparency | _Your analysis_ |
| | Academic integrity | _Your analysis_ |
| | Data/privacy issues | _Your analysis_ |
| | Resource constraints | _Your analysis_ |

- Identify **key stakeholders** (internal and external) and explain **why they matter**.

| Stakeholder | Internal/External | Interest/Influence | Why They Matter |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| _e.g., Academic staff_ | Internal | _High interest, high influence_ | _Your analysis_ |
| _e.g., Students_ | Internal | _High interest, moderate influence_ | _Your analysis_ |
| _e.g., External regulators_ | External | _Moderate interest, high influence_ | _Your analysis_ |

#### 3. Implementation Strategy (~700–800 words)

Use **Kotter's 8-Step Model**, **Lewin's Change Model** (Unfreeze → Change → Refreeze), or another suitable framework.

Your strategy should cover (where relevant):

- [ ] Creating a strong **rationale and sense of urgency**
- [ ] Building support through **stakeholder engagement and coalition-building**
- [ ] Communicating a **clear vision** and keeping people informed
- [ ] Managing **resistance** in a realistic way
- [ ] Maintaining **momentum** and embedding the change
- [ ] Considering **politics, power dynamics**, and practical constraints

**Note:** Words in tables do **not** count towards the word limit. Use tables to summarise key points concisely, but ensure they align with the overall narrative.
