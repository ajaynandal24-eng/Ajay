## Component 3: Change Management Plan (50%)

**Suggested length: ~1,500 words**

### Additional Evidence

![Additional Evidence](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ffaa96ac-ed6f-4423-bfc8-c31b3a968240)

### The Case

> ARU is considering adopting AI to mark student coursework assignments and exams. As part of a consultancy team, you have been asked to design an organisational change plan to inform ARU's decision and help guide implementation.

### Suggested Structure

#### 1. Introduction (~150–200 words)

- Briefly state the **purpose of the plan**: to develop a structured change management strategy for the adoption of AI-assisted marking at ARU.
- Define what a **"successful" implementation** would look like — e.g., a phased rollout that maintains academic standards, gains staff buy-in, ensures transparency for students, and builds institutional capacity.

#### 2. Enablers and Barriers (~400–500 words)

Use **Lewin's Force Field Analysis** (recommended) or another suitable framework.

| Category | Factor | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Enablers (Driving Forces)** | Institutional readiness | ARU's existing digital infrastructure and strategic openness to innovation |
| | Consistency and speed | AI can provide faster, more consistent marking across large cohorts |
| | Reduced marking burden | Frees academic staff time for teaching, feedback, and research |
| | Student benefits | Quicker turnaround on results; potentially more detailed/consistent feedback |
| | Capability building | Opportunity to develop staff digital skills and AI literacy |
| **Barriers (Restraining Forces)** | Staff resistance | Concerns about job security, loss of professional autonomy, and scepticism about AI accuracy |
| | Ethical concerns | Bias in AI algorithms, fairness, and transparency of marking criteria |
| | Academic integrity | Risk that AI cannot assess critical thinking, creativity, or nuanced argumentation |
| | Trust and transparency | Students and staff may distrust machine-generated grades |
| | Data/privacy issues | Handling of student work and personal data under GDPR |
| | Resource constraints | Cost of procurement, training, and ongoing maintenance |
| | Workload pressures | Additional burden during transition period |

- Identify **key stakeholders** (internal and external) and explain **why they matter**.

| Stakeholder | Internal/External | Interest/Influence | Why They Matter |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Academic staff (lecturers, markers) | Internal | High interest, high influence | Directly affected; their buy-in is critical for adoption |
| Students | Internal | High interest, moderate influence | Primary recipients of AI-marked feedback |
| Senior leadership / Vice-Chancellor | Internal | Moderate interest, high influence | Control strategic direction and resource allocation |
| IT / Digital Services | Internal | Moderate interest, high influence | Responsible for technical implementation and support |
| Quality Assurance team | Internal | High interest, high influence | Must ensure standards are maintained |
| Professional bodies / Accreditors | External | Moderate interest, high influence | Set standards that ARU must meet |
| AI technology vendors | External | High interest, moderate influence | Provide the tools and ongoing technical support |
| Office for Students (OfS) | External | Moderate interest, high influence | Regulatory oversight of HE quality |

#### 3. Implementation Strategy (~700–800 words)

Use **Kotter's 8-Step Model** or **Lewin's Change Model** (Unfreeze → Change → Refreeze), or combine both.

**Kotter's 8 Steps applied to AI marking at ARU:**

| Step | Action | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1. Create urgency | Highlight pressures | Growing student numbers, marking turnaround expectations, sector-wide AI adoption trends |
| 2. Build a guiding coalition | Engage key stakeholders | Form a working group of academics, IT, QA, students, and senior leaders |
| 3. Form a strategic vision | Define the goal | AI as a *support tool* for markers, not a replacement — maintaining human oversight |
| 4. Communicate the vision | Transparent comms | Town halls, staff briefings, student forums, FAQ documents |
| 5. Remove barriers | Address resistance | Training programmes, pilot results sharing, addressing ethical concerns openly |
| 6. Generate short-term wins | Pilot programme | Trial AI marking in low-stakes assessments (e.g., formative MCQs) before expanding |
| 7. Sustain acceleration | Expand gradually | Phased rollout across departments, continuous feedback loops |
| 8. Anchor the change | Embed in culture | Update policies, integrate into staff development, regular review cycles |

Your strategy narrative should cover:

- Creating a strong **rationale and sense of urgency** — why now, what happens if ARU doesn't act
- Building support through **stakeholder engagement and coalition-building** — who needs to be involved and how
- Communicating a **clear vision** — AI as an aid, not a replacement; maintaining academic standards
- Managing **resistance** in a realistic way — acknowledging valid concerns, providing training and reassurance
- Maintaining **momentum** and embedding the change — from pilot to policy
- Considering **politics, power dynamics**, and practical constraints — union concerns, budget limitations, competing priorities
